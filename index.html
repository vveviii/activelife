What is a sedentary lifestyle?


Sedentary lifestyle, more commonly known as inactive lifestyle refers

to a lifestyle with little to no physical activity. People are spending more

and more time doing sedentary activites, especially during the COVID-19 pandemic.

Most of our jobs have become more sedentary, with long days sitting at a desk.






Health risks of an inactive lifestyle


Sedentary lifestyles increase all causes of mortality, double the risk of cardiovascular diseases,

diabetes, and obesity, and increase the risks of colon cancer, high blood pressure, osteoporosis,

lipid disorders, depression and anxiety. According to WHO, 60 to 85% of people in the world—from both

developed and developing countries—lead sedentary lifestyles, making it one of the more serious yet insufficiently

addressed public health problems of our time. It is estimated that nearly two-thirds of children are also insufficiently

active, with serious implications for their future health.
